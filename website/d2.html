Getting Started with Your Drone

Types of Drones

RTF (Ready To Fly)

You'll be able to fly it more or less straight out of the box. Just charge the batteries and perhaps put on the rotor blades.

BNF (Bind-N-Fly)

Sold as complete quadcopters but don't include the controller. You need to buy a separate handset controller.

ARF (Almost Ready to Fly)

You will need to work on the drone before flying. This can mean assembling it or adding missing components.

Anatomy of a Drone

Battery

Typically only powers the drone for 20 minutes per charge.

Rotors

Shaped like aircraft wings to produce lift as air flows over them.

GPS Aerial

With GPS your drone knows exactly where it is and makes decisions based on that.

Booms

The arms of your drone must be light but strong and thin enough to not interfere with rotor downdraft.

Essential Safety Tips

  • Ensure you never fly your drone near people's bodies and faces
  • Switch off the drone's motor before the controller handset
  • Remove the rotors when you're not using the UAV
  • Don't risk flying unless weather conditions are good
  • Follow instructions for recharging Lithium Polymer batteries
