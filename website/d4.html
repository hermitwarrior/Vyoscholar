Photographing & Filming with Drones

Drone filming is big news in media today. Almost every time you watch the news, there will be great sequences showing buildings and places from angles that would be impossible without drones.

Professional Camera Moves

Tracking Shot

Gently move your drone past an object with the camera set at 45 degrees. The object appears in shot, then steadily moves out as you pass by.

Panning

Swivel the camera horizontally while the drone stays still, just as you would turn your head to follow a passing car.

Tilting

Take the camera from pointing at the horizon and slowly rotate it downwards, like looking over a cliff edge.

Reveal Shot

Start looking vertically downwards, then tilt upwards to show the subject - a classic Hollywood move.

Pre-Roll

Roll the camera for 5 seconds before starting your move, and hold for 5 seconds after. This gives editors "headroom".

Story First

Even simple drone sequences should tell a story. Plan how your shots will flow together before filming.
