Drone Racing

Drone racing is the new form of aerial combat, and it's big. A unique combination of PC gaming, gladiatorial motorsport, Hollywood movie effects and adrenaline.

Getting Started in Racing

Nearly all drones used for racing have quad rotors. They are lightweight, stripped-down machines with the absolute bare minimum of equipment. Every drone-racing pilot offers the same advice: start with a small, inexpensive drone like the Hubsan X4.

FPV - First Person View

At the heart of drone racing excitement is FPV (First-Person View). Each drone has an on-board, fixed camera which transmits back a live video signal to your flying goggles. Because the camera is fixed and not gyro stabilized, you experience the full effect of acceleration and the roll of the horizon as you bank, twist and turn around the course.

Anatomy of a Racing Quad

Frame

Typically all carbon. Popular models include the Blackout Mini H and the Lumenier QAV250.

Flight Controller

Most popular are the Naze32 Acro and the OpenPilot CC3D.

Motors and Props

Cobra engines with HQ Props are premium brands. Budget options include Sunny Sky motors with GenFan props.

Battery Packs

All Lithium Polymer. Turnigy Nano-Tech cells allow very high discharge rates needed for racing.

How Racing Works

Rotorcross

Flat-out, side-by-side drone racing with multiple pilots forming a grid around a pre-set course.

Drone Dragster

Two drones compete in a standing start drag race down a 100 meter track.

Time Trial

Fly individually against the clock, with the winner holding the fastest stage time.
